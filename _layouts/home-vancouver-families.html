How to Play

#1 Assemble your team!

The number one most rhyming rule for apocalypse survival is don't go solo. So register a team. Gather 1 to 4 other people. Then invite them to join you by sharing your unique team code.

How to Play

#2 Choose scenario!

Pick from four apocalyptic scenarios in Vancouver. Bring yourselves and your phones to its start point to get… started. (We recommend families begin with the EARTHQUAKE PREPAREDNESS scenario, which starts outside of Science World!)

How to Play

#3 Start playing!

Once you arrive, go to this website (play.apocalypsemadeeasy.com) to start the scenario. A somewhat-helpful AI named BORIS will send instructions directly to your phones.

How to Play

#4 Win!

Or lose. Both are equally likely. Just know that the stakes are high: top teams will win highly-fashionable survival prize packs.

Remember!

- Each team member needs a phone (of course—there’s nothing to say that phones can’t be shared!)

FAQS

  • How does it work?

    Create or join a team of 2 to 5 people. Then choose from four different scenarios, each focusing on a separate aspect of doomsday preparedness. Take your phones and travel to the scenario's starting point. An AI named BORIS will send your first instructions straight to your phone’s web browser.

    Work together to interpret BORIS' confounding statements, complete challenges, and survive the scenario. And do all of that accurately and promptly, as you’re being judged and timed.

  • Where can I do it?

    It's available in Vancouver in the locations marked on the map above me here. There are scenarios at Stanley Park, the False Creek Seawall, UBC, and Pacific Spirit Park.

  • What's it cost?

    It's free! But the true cost is if you don’t participate and are ill-prepared for the end. That cost is astronomical.

  • Do I need to download an app?

    No app download is needed! It runs in your phone's internet browser, such as Safari or Chrome. But it will use around 5 MB of mobile data, which is similar to streaming one song.
